                                                   SRI LANKA

On March 17th 2005, Nations Reach team went to visit Sri Lanka. Our mission partners from Nuewralia were waiting for us in Colombo. Our plan was to visit the worst hit Tsunami areas in Sri Lanka. The purpose of this trip was to identify the need for workers and to prepare project proposals for Nations Reach to consider future developments.

March17, 2005

On March 17, 2005 I left India on Jet Airways at 12 p.m. and headed towards Sri Lanka.

After arriving in Sri Lanka, we traveled by car to go to Kalmunai via Kandy. I met with Pastor Selvarajah at 11.30 p.m after a 9 hour drive from the airport.

Had a meeting with all the local workers and went to view several Tsunami disaster areas;
  • Visited the most effected areas in Sri Lanka.
  • Went through army restricted areas to see how the camps have been setup.
  • Visited camps controlled by the TASK FORCE.
  • Visited camps controlled by LTTE.
  • Had a meeting with local Little Roses staffs, and potential future workers for Little Roses.
  • Met with Kannan who is in need of support for his work, and a church building that was completely destroyed by the Tsunami.
  • Had a discussion with Selvarajah for our future leadership conference and partnership in missions.
Handed over check for Tsunami relief work.


                                                   ANDAMAN

Andaman, a beautiful pearl of an island situated in the midst of Indian Ocean just below the coastal line of Burma. Nations Reach's object was to identify the children and orphans affected by the Tsunami disaster, and to work with local missions to form partnership for future ministries.

When we went to see the work of different ministries, we were surprised to see that truly the harvest is plentiful but the laborers are few. Now, this has challenged us in Nations Reach to consider having our mission's base on this island. We got into action to see the possibilities of recruiting missionaries and build churches. The result now is that we have completely adopted a local mission agency with 15 missionaries, and to start building two churches. We have also come up with a plan to hold leadership conferences every year here on this island. Our first Coastal Hope Christian Leadership Conference will be held on the 3rd week of July 2005.

There will be a team consisting of 7 leaders from the US who will be conducting a 3 day leadership conference at Port Blair in Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Our immediate needs for this island are; one motorbike, one jeep and monthly support for our workers. We will be starting our Little Roses Children's Home soon here on this island.

NATIONS NEWS UPDATE APRIL2005

  • Little Roses Building in Rajapalayam, S.India

    This building project though was completed and dedicated in April 2004, had a lot of pending minor and major furnishing and maintenance jobs. This got delayed due to lack of funds. Praise God! This March, nearly 11 months after the dedication we were able to see this come through. Children living here are very happy with this provision of fans and mosquito mesh for windows, tiled bathrooms, toilets, overhead water tank, plumbing works, a kitchen sink, and sewage line.

    Surely this home has become a place of providing a homely atmosphere for our children to study and enjoy their childhood. Yes, these kids have tasted the goodness of the Lord this year!


  • Little Roses Building in Dindigul, S.India - Ground Breaking

    The Lord has answered the cry and petition of these children and has provided a very suitable land 5 kms away from the present home easily accessible to the road in the month of March 2005. The Little Roses Home that has been functioning in a rented building for nearly 8years and caring for over 55 children has crossed a milestone in seeing a breakthrough for a permanent building.

    We launched our second building Little Rose Home construction by initially digging a bore well at 350 feet for water source to go ahead with the planned & proposed 1300 sq feet building project. The initial ground breaking for the home was done on 18th March 2005. This home will be completed in five months and God willing, be ready for dedication at the end of September 2005.


  • Little Roses Home in Ooty, India:

    Plans are underway for Little Roses Children's Home project to cater to the needs of 20-25 tribal children and special needs children living in the hill station of the Nilgiris district. The proposed site for this project is ideal for a children's home because the land is adjacent to the high school. The scope of reaching into several villages with the light of the gospel through the existing school, and our other ministries especially the proposed children's home could make a difference here in Agalar. (This place is approximately 15 kms away from Ooty town and is surrounded by nearly 15-20 villages).

    The Nilgiris is renowned for its hill tribes. It is occupied by distinct tribes of which the Todas, the Kotas, the Kurumbas, and the Irulas are considered to be primitive and aboriginal. Paniyas , Kasavas , Mullukurumbas ,Chetties, Shola Naickens, Malai Naickens are other hill tribes living in Nilgiris.


  • Andamans & Nicobar Islands :

    Recently, Raja Sekar and the Nation Reach team visited Andamans & Nicobar Islands to survey and determine the felt need to help the recent Tsunami victims. They recall it to be a timely visit. We are coming up with a special initiative with missions to Andamans & Nicobar Island to help rehabilitate about 50 children later this year. This will depend on the responses of donors and partners.

    Due to the recent Tsunami devastation, our mercy ministry is expanding and we are looking beyond our horizon to Sri Lanka especially in the coming year of 2006. Staffs are already ready to help establish a children's home project for 50 children affected by the Tsunami.


  • Our Goal :


    • The children's home project is growing gradually and steadily. Our goal is to help approximately 200 to 250 children. Though the need is great in this part of the world, we want to be realistic and offer quality education, provide good homely atmosphere, accommodations and care to help these children grow in the fear of the Lord, empower them to rise and shine for Jesus, to glorify God and ultimately be a blessing TO THE NATIONS.


MISSIONS : FOR SUCH A TIME AS THIS

NATIONS REACH'S PASTORS' CONFERENCE AND EVENING GOSPEL CRUSADE (Post Tsunami Christian leaders conference in Andaman);

This year the pastors' conference will be held in Andaman & Nicobar Islands. One of the observed and felt needs during Raja Sekar's visit to Andaman in March 2005 was to offer encouragement, renewal, and revival to Christian workers and pastors who were devastated and affected by the tsunami. Many had lost their loved ones, belongings, homes and even church buildings.

Therefore, we are planning for a three-day conference for Christian leaders, pastors, and workers in Port Blair, Andaman. The target is for 250-300 participants. The goal is to have an evening gospel meeting to encourage local Christians and also to reach the lost for a great harvest of souls.

The conference will be held in Megapode Nest, Port Blair, and the tentative dates are from July 26th to 28th 2005. The evening gospel crusade/meeting will be held at Dr. Ambedkar Auditorium.

Did you know that the Andaman & Nicobar Islands have 28 zip code areas, 547 villages, nearly 49 inhabited islands with 26 different language groups, and 6 tribal groups?

SUPPORTING NATIVE MISSIONARIES

One of the goals for Nations Reach Network is to support native missionaries, pastors, and Christian workers through input seminars, conferences, trainings, sending our own gospel and ministry teams, and through minimum financial support so that they would not give up but continue to give themselves to serve the Lord.

At present, there are approximately 10-15 Christian pastors and workers receiving some financial support through Nations Reach Network.
